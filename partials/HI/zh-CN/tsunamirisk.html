Tsunami Risk

Knowledge is safety. To reduce your risk, know your tsunami facts.

What is a tsunami?

A tsunami(Japenese for "harbor waves") is a series of ocean waves produced by a sudden rise or fall in the earth's crust, most commonly caused by an earthquake or underwater landslide.

In the open ocean, tsunami waves cannot be seen or felt by ships or airplanes because the water are actually hundreds of miles wide, with a height of only a few feet. But as the waves approach the coast, their height increases dramatically and can be very destructive when they reach the shore.

Local Tsunamis vs. Distant Source Tsuanmis

Locally generated tsunamis can arrive at the coast within minutes, even before a warning can be issued.

All Islands are also vulnerable to distant source tsunamis. These tsunamis resul from distant earthquakes and/or landslides in places like Chile, Alaska, and Japan, and can arrive within hours.
