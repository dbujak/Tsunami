Tsunami Plan Ahead

You cannot prevent a tsunami, but you can be prepared for one. Actions you take now could save your life and the lives of your loved ones.

Develop a Family Emergency Plan

Prepare yourself and your family for a disaster by making an emergency plan. Your family may not be together when disaster strikes, so it is important to plan in advance.

Create your Family Emergency Plan, and visity the Hawaii State Civil Defense website for mor preparedness information.

Don't forget about your pets

Have preparations in place to care fo your pets in case you are ordered to evacuate.

Assemble and Emergency Preparedness Kit

Emergency preparedness kits should contain the essentials your family needs to survive during a disaster. It may take 72 hours or mor for the emergency personnel to reach you.

Recommendations for a Disaster Preparedness Kit

Plan Ahead

Become familiar with potential evacuation routes, develop and evacuation plan, and update it when issues arise.

Practice your evacuation plan at least twice a year with your family so when there is a tsunami, you are prepared to act!

Keep in mind hundreds of families in your area share the same concerns, and it will be difficult to access the necessities you need due to shortages and competition.

Sign up to be included in your local emergency notification system

if you are hearing impaired, sign up for the Sprint Relay Hawaii text notification tool.
