Tsunami Warning

Know the Natural Warning Signs of a Tsunami - use your senses!

FEEL

Feel the ground shaking severly?

Head for higher ground. Strong local earthquakes may cause tsunamis.

SEE

See an unusual dissappearance of water, or an oncoming wall of water?

Immediately leave low-lying coastal areas and move inland. As a tsunami approaches the shoreline, sea levels often recede, exposing the ocean floor, reef, and fish

HEAR

Hear the roar?

An approaching tsunami may create a loud "roaring", similar to the sound of a moving train or jet aircraft.

Emergency Information

The Hawai'i State Emergency Alert System (EAS) is the mechanism used to notify the public of a possible approaching tsunami.

Listen for the steady three minute siren tone (this is the attention alert signal).

Turn on the nearest radio or television and listen for emergency information and instruction.

As part of the EAS, designated radio and local television broadcasting statsion were selected as primary sources for emergency information.
